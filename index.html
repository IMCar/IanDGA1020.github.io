Biography


My name is Ian Mark Caruana , 39 years old with great interest in sound design especially natural organic soundscapes. A passion for Digital Arts sparked since I felt the need to colour my compositions with a visual narrative. Whilst producing music and soundscapes my mind starts to create pictures and sometimes I can also visualize a story which takes over my composition driving the sounds being produced. In 2021 I decide to join the Department of Digital Arts at the University of Malta which has been an exceptional learning experience.

Sound design is the art of giving aural texture to visuals.

Music and sound production was once considered as a post visual process. This is a normal proceedure in the production of traditional filming. The sound design process is nowadays being initiated by many production companies at concept phase especially in the creation of animation and games. Sound and music is like a binding agent which moulds the visuals. Sound may alter the viewers understanding of the visual dynamics in a scene. If your hear the sound of glass shattering, what does this trigger in your thoughts? The breaking sound could have many direct relations to the breaking of an actual object made of glass but it can also trigger an emotional condition that something irreversable has happened such as the end of a relationship.

I personally think of sound and music to be a complete phenomena because one can visualize his own creative story in his imagination. Visuals on their own effect our reasoning of happenings but visuals with sound, effect our senses deeper giving more weight, volume and dynamics to a scene. Imagine you are watching a clip of a gunshot or a bomb exploding or else a high speed car crash without sound. Adding sound to the 3 examples will add understanding such as the type of gun being used, the severity and dimensions of the explosion, the speed and severity of the impact.

Currently I am working with various visual design tools and experiencing a well-structured tuition by experts in the field. My main interest is in the production of moving graphics such as film, animation and 3d.

The projects presented underneath are a selection of completed assignments using various design tools The techniques used to achieve the results were primarily researched and later applied. In some of the projects more than one software and technique was used in order to learn how the practices interect together.

Title: Fruit Bowl

3D Sculpting/Animation

The fruit and bowl in this animation were created by using the Scupting tool in Blender. The most difficult part of this excercise was to give texture to different fruit especially the strawbery. Different techniques were used in the same software to achieve a realistic result resembling a sculpture. Adding different levels of white light and varying positions and intesity of the light created shadows and reflections outlining the different shapes and positioning.

Title: Fruity Pendular

Videography/Sound Production/Video and sound Editing

The creative approach taken towards this project was to experiment with light, transparent objects and colours in a still life videography experience. The fruit were used for their vibrant colours and shapes thus helping in creating depth in the set whilst wine glasses were used to create reflections and visual effects. As the name defines their is a pendulum which seems floating from one side to the other expressing tha notion of movement and time. Without the pendulum and another moving object in the end of the video the filming could have been a single photograph. The process and text were edited in Adobe Premiere Pro whilst the soundscape and sound fx were self produced and mastered in Ableton Live 11.

Title: Earth Worm

Pixel Art/Unity animation

Pixel art is famous for its use in games and the idea behind this kind of art is to create charaters that need not be close to real. You do not need to be an experienced artist to create your own character and I chose to create an Earth Worm as test ground to be used in Unity game engine. The earth worm is seen performing the Idle, Walk, Jump, Run, Fall and Dying sequences used in most 2d platform games. Piskel is a very simple online application were one can create his own Pixel art and extract the image sequences called sprites to be used in game engines or animation software.

Title: Saxophone Player

Photogrometry/3D sculpting and animation

I wanted to explore the possibilities of using Photogrometry software such as 3DF Zephyr to capture a 3D scan of an object. Photogrometry is the process of creating a 3D drawing of an object in software by using photos taken from diffenet angles. For this project 55 photos were taken. The sofware will than do complex calculations from the photos uploaded and renders a 3 dimentional image as a result. The software creates a 3D mesh model of the object that can later be processed in other 3D software such as Blender. A pedestal and flooring were added to the animation to give more dynamics to the rotating object.
